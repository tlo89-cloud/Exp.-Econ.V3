Six Converging Forces
Growing Wealth Gap
K-Curve economy drives demand at every price point
The K-Curve economy creates demand for premium/luxury experiences at the top, affordable alternatives in the middle, and budget options for consumers priced out of tier-one events.
Contracted Revenue
70%+ of team revenue locked via media deals
Major sports leagues (MLB, NFL, NBA) have contracted revenues for 10+ years. Over 70% of a team's revenue comes from media deals regardless of on-field performance or the economic cycle.
Social Media
Generational shift from goods to experiences
A generational shift toward spending on experiences rather than material goods, originally with millennials and Gen Z, is now broadening across age groups.
Digital Counterweight
Physical presence grows as AI saturates digital life
As AI and technology penetrate more parts of daily life - health, work, and education - the value of being physically present grows as a counterweight to digital immersion.
Shorter Work Weeks
More leisure time = more demand for experiences
A structural trend toward fewer working hours creates more leisure time. AI is expected to accelerate this. We went from 7-day work weeks to 5 days. 4 days is inevitable.
Physical Scarcity
One place, one time - a scarce resource
Attending a live experience requires being in a specific place at a specific time. This is a tangible, scarce resource in a world where almost everything else is abundant and digital.

Layer 2 of 5
Physical Infrastructure
Intellectual property is why people show up; physical infrastructure determines how many can show up, what they pay, and what the experience feels like. This is primarily a real estate layer. The business model is being rebuilt - legacy venues were designed around seat count and concessions, while modern venues are designed around premium hospitality, sponsorship integration, and technology-first operations. The goal for new builds is to have a venue that operates 350+ days per year.
Sub-Segments
Stadiums & Arenas
The average major US arena is 25-30 years old and designed for a different era. New builds are engineered as technology and hospitality platforms first, arenas second. CapEx-heavy, long-duration assets with predictable cash flows tied to scarce locations.

Market Dynamics
Rebuild Supercycle
Aging venue stock forces a generational wave of new builds - ~$200B in new build pipeline. This dynamic applies across segments where legacy concepts (Dave & Busters, TopGolf) are going out of fashion, making room for modern concepts.
Technology Embedded in Real Estate
The shift from static buildings to smart, data-driven venues is structural. Technology drives incremental revenue that justifies $2B+ CapEx. Unlike most real estate, venue technology creates compounding returns as data gets richer and personalization improves.
Premiumization Across Every Format
Stadiums add suites and clubs. Sphere charges $200-1,000+. Disney raises park prices annually. Competitive socializing positions above a traditional bar. Consumers have a high willingness to pay for premium physical experiences.
Initial View
Our Take
The rebuild cycle is a multi-decade tailwind. Aging venues force construction, and every new build generates more revenue per square foot for 30-40 years. Technology permanently changes what this real estate can produce. From a macro perspective, this layer seems very interesting - within real estate, these projects serve as a premium asset class.

Overview

We define the Experience Economy as any business that creates, enables, or monetizes live events that consumers participate in or attend - sports, concerts, festivals, comedy, immersive attractions, hybrid experiences, and the technology that powers them.

This economy is entering a multi-decade expansion. Our view is that this sector is directionally underpriced and in 2026 we are unable to even imagine what live experiences will look like a decade from today.

Five Converging Forces
→Growing Wealth Gap - K-Curve economy creates demand for premium experiences at the top, affordable alternatives in the middle, and budget options for consumers priced out of tier-one events.
→Contracted Revenue - Major sports leagues have contracted revenues for 10+ years. Over 70% of a team's revenue comes from media deals regardless of on-field performance or the economic cycle.
→Social Media - A generational shift toward spending on experiences rather than material goods, originally with millennials and Gen Z, is now broadening across age groups.
→Digital Counterweight - As AI and technology penetrate daily life, the value of being physically present grows as a counterweight to digital immersion.
→Shorter Work Weeks - A structural trend toward fewer working hours creates more leisure time. We went from 7 days to 5 days. 4 is inevitable.
→Physical Scarcity - A specific place at a specific time. Tangible and scarce in a world where almost everything else is abundant and digital.
Layer-by-Layer
1 - Intellectual Property

Everything is downstream of IP. The scarcity aspect will continue to drive asset values upward. It's hard to create new IP overnight. In an attention-scarce world, affection for the underlying IP drives spending. Top-tier assets will command more value over time.

2 - Physical Infrastructure

The rebuild cycle is a multi-decade tailwind. Aging venues force construction and every new build generates more revenue per square foot for 30–40 years. Technology is becoming intertwined - it drives incremental revenue that justifies $2B+ CapEx.

3 - Operators / Access / Distribution

Consolidation is a dominant theme. Vertical integration wins - the more touchpoints controlled, the more margin captured. Discovery remains the highest-upside, most untapped position in the entire layer.

4 - Service Providers

Necessary but not obviously attractive. Grows with the experience economy but captures thin margins. Exceptions: companies with exclusive rights to marquee events or control over talent pipelines have built real positions.

5 - Technology Enablers

The challenge is separating durable platforms from point solutions. Sports data and betting infrastructure is the clearest investable sub-segment today. Fan engagement has been "emerging" for a decade without a breakout. Venue tech is tied to the rebuild cycle.
